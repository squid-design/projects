We're building an Espresso Calculator

To all baristas and coffee professionals who want to achieve the perfect extraction under any conditions, it’s time to take the first step. Current trial-and-error (pull and sip) techniques are inefficient and outdated: so Matt Perger & Analytical Flavor Systems have teamed up to create the first "big data" Espresso Calculator.

Using machine learning, artificial intelligence, and the sensors on your smart phone, we will mesure and understand the decisions and variables you can control to pull the perfect shot.

A unique fingerprint for every shot

Every shot is an individual snowflake, and it is impossible to map out a shot of espresso and replicate it. Each model of espresso machine is built differently and calibrated for specific cafe settings. By pulling metadata from baristas all over the world, along with espresso reviews, we will be able to fingerprint every shot pulled and help you achieve the desired flavor profile.

We need your help!

We are building a calculator that can be used to create a consistently awesome espresso experience at your café. In the future, baristas can pull a shot of espresso, record the flavor profile and paremeters, and recive actionable feedback on how to adjust time, temperature, grind, and dose to optmiize the characteristics that are desired from your single origin or espresso blend.

We need your help to make this happen - "big data" techniques requier data - your data. To build this predictive espresso optimization calculator, we're asking hundreds of baristas like you to pull three shots of espresso in one highly caffinated session, and review that espresso on their smart phone using Gastrograph Review.

Introducing Gastrograph

Gastrograph is a flavor profiling system developed by AFS to quantify flavor in complex beverages. Gastrograph uses 18 broad spectrum flavor categories and eight sensations (trigeminal & somatosensory) that fully encompass gustatory flavor space; such that anything you can taste, you can graph. That gives us 24 different flavor profiles that you to rate on an intensity scale of 0-5, and then you assign a perceived quality score of the product. Gastrograph allows you to compare objective flavor profiles of consumer and quality control-based reviews on all of your products in order to determine Correlates of Quality, consumer preference, and analyze changes in products over time.

    Weighted Average PQ : 3.2
  • More roasted notes
  • More notable body
  • Richer flavor
    Weighted Average PQ : 2.2
  • Less "pop"
  • Less mouthfeel, more watery consistency
  • Same amount of bitterness and retronasal as fine grind
  • Overall lower quality extraction

Where do we go from here?
